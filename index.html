About Cancer in children

Childhood cancer constitutes 3 to 4 % of all cancers. Every year 4 lakh children all over the world get diagnosed with cancer, out of which India has an estimated 76805 cases of new childhood cancer cases detected every year. Unlike adult cancers, the causes of most childhood cancers are as yet unknown but in developed countries more than 80% of childhood cancer is curable because of better response to the treatment and protocols. Despite these high cure rates in developed countries, it is estimated that in India, less than 30% of children get cured largely due to lack of awareness, late detection and late diagnosis, inadequate facilities, the high cost of treatment and the absence of supportive care.​Click Here to know more

Our journey so far

Started in October 2021, Amogh Foundation has been engaging with cancer hospitals in Delhi for concessional treatments to poor children and also raising funds for the same. Currently while treatment costs varies differently for different types of cancer, on an average the concessional rate of treatment works out to approx. Rs. 1.50 lakhs for radiation/ surgery, Rs. 3.00 lakhs for Chemotherapy per child (who are below poverty line) as against average cost of Rs.4.5 lakh to Rs.6 lakhs. During the last 4 months, Amogh Foundation has raised funds and helped fund the surgery of a 5 year old child battling pelvic Bone cancer (Ewing’s Sarcoma) as a part of her cancer treatment.
